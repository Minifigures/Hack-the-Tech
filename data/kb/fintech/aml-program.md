# BSA / AML Program

## five-pillars

The Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) requires every covered financial institution to
maintain a written, board-approved Anti-Money Laundering (AML) program. The
program must include:

1. A designated BSA compliance officer with the authority, independence, and
   resources necessary to administer the program.
2. A system of internal controls and written policies that ensure ongoing
   compliance.
3. Ongoing AML training for relevant employees.
4. Independent testing of the program by qualified personnel.
5. Customer due diligence (CDD), including beneficial ownership identification
   under FinCEN's 2018 CDD rule.

## sars

Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) must be filed within 30 calendar days of
detecting a transaction suspected of violating law or BSA, with a 30-day
extension permitted if no suspect is identified. SARs are confidential and
must not be disclosed to the subject.

## ctrs

Currency Transaction Reports are required for cash transactions exceeding
$10,000 in aggregate per business day per person. Structuring to evade the
threshold is itself a violation.
