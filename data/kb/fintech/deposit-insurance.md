# Deposit Insurance

## fdic-limits

Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) insurance covers up to $250,000
per depositor, per FDIC-insured bank, per ownership category. Recognised
ownership categories include:

- Single accounts.
- Joint accounts (each co-owner is insured up to $250,000).
- Certain retirement accounts (e.g., IRAs).
- Revocable trust accounts (per beneficiary, up to limits).
- Irrevocable trust accounts.
- Employee benefit plan accounts.

A depositor with funds across multiple ownership categories at the same bank
can therefore be insured for more than $250,000 in aggregate at a single
institution.

## ncua

National Credit Union Administration share insurance mirrors FDIC at the
$250,000 per share owner, per insured credit union, per ownership category
level. The Share Insurance Fund stands behind these guarantees.
