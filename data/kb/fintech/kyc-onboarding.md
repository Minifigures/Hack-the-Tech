# KYC and Customer Onboarding

## sole-proprietor

A US sole proprietor opening a business deposit account is treated as an
individual customer plus a doing-business-as (DBA) record. The customer
identification program (CIP) package typically consists of:

- Government-issued photo identification for the owner.
- The owner's Social Security Number (SSN).
- The business legal name and any DBA / trade name.
- Employer Identification Number (EIN), or SSN if the business uses the
  owner's SSN as its tax identifier.
- Documentation of the business address (utility bill, lease, or signed
  attestation).
- Business license, professional license, or DBA filing where applicable.

The institution must also perform OFAC and PEP screening, and form an internal
risk rating from the CIP package.

## llc-or-corporation

For US legal entity customers (LLC, C-corp, S-corp), the institution must
collect the beneficial owner (each natural person owning 25 percent or more
equity) and one control person under FinCEN's beneficial ownership rule.

## enhanced-due-diligence

EDD applies to higher-risk customers: foreign financial institutions,
non-resident aliens, money services businesses, and politically exposed
persons. EDD includes additional documentation, source-of-funds inquiry, and
senior management approval of the relationship.
