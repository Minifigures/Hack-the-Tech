# Lending Compliance

## adverse-action

An adverse action notice is required under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act
(ECOA) and the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) when a creditor takes adverse
action against a credit applicant. Adverse action includes:

- Denying credit.
- Approving credit in an amount or on terms less favorable than requested.
- Failing to make an offer in response to a counter-offer.

The notice must disclose the specific principal reasons for the adverse action
and, when based on a consumer report, must identify the reporting agency and
include the consumer's rights under the FCRA. The notice must be delivered
within 30 days of the credit decision.

## fair-lending

Fair lending laws (ECOA, FHA, HMDA) prohibit discrimination on prohibited
bases: race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age,
receipt of public assistance, or the good-faith exercise of consumer rights.
Disparate treatment, disparate impact, and overt discrimination are all
actionable.

## hmda-reporting

Home Mortgage Disclosure Act reporters must report loan-application records
(LAR) annually with applicant, geography, loan, and decision fields. Errors
above CFPB tolerances trigger resubmission and may result in civil money
penalties.
