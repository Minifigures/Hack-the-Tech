# Retirement Account Rules

## roth-ira-2025

For tax year 2025, the Roth IRA contribution limit is $7,000 for filers under
age 50 and $8,000 for filers age 50 and over. Direct Roth IRA contributions
phase out based on Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI):

- Single and head of household: phase-out range is $150,000 to $165,000.
  Above $165,000 MAGI, direct contributions are not permitted.
- Married filing jointly: phase-out range is $236,000 to $246,000.

A taxpayer above the limit may make a nondeductible contribution to a
traditional IRA and convert it to a Roth IRA (the "backdoor Roth"), subject to
the IRS pro-rata aggregation rule across all of the taxpayer's traditional,
SEP, and SIMPLE IRAs.

## 401k-2025

The 2025 employee elective deferral limit for 401(k), 403(b), and most 457
plans is $23,500. Catch-up contributions for participants age 50 and over add
$7,500. SECURE 2.0 introduces an enhanced catch-up of $11,250 for participants
ages 60 through 63.

## rmd

Required Minimum Distributions begin at age 73 under SECURE 2.0 for
individuals who attain age 73 after 2022. The first RMD may be delayed to
April 1 of the year following the year the participant reaches the
applicable age, but subsequent RMDs must be taken by December 31 each year.
