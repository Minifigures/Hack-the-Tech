# Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) Controls

## section-302

Section 302 requires the principal executive and financial officers of a
public company to personally certify, on each quarterly and annual report, the
accuracy of the financial statements and the effectiveness of the disclosure
controls.

## section-404

Section 404 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act requires public companies to document
and assess the effectiveness of their internal controls over financial
reporting (ICFR). Each annual report must include:

1. A statement by management acknowledging responsibility for establishing and
   maintaining an adequate ICFR structure.
2. Management's assessment of the effectiveness of ICFR as of the end of the
   fiscal year, including disclosure of any material weaknesses.
3. An attestation report from the registered public accounting firm on the
   effectiveness of ICFR.

Smaller reporting companies and non-accelerated filers are exempt from the
external auditor attestation requirement but still file management assessments.

## section-906

Section 906 requires a separate certification of periodic reports containing
financial statements; knowingly false certifications carry criminal penalties.

## remediation

Material weaknesses identified during a SOX assessment trigger a remediation
plan with owner, target date, and an interim mitigating control. Remediation
status is reported quarterly to the Disclosure Committee.
