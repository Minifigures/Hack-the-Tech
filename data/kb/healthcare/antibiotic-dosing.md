# Antibiotic Dosing Reference

## amoxicillin-aom

For uncomplicated acute otitis media (AOM) in adults, first-line therapy is
amoxicillin 500 mg orally every 8 hours, or 875 mg orally every 12 hours, for 5
to 7 days. In regions with high pneumococcal resistance, high-dose amoxicillin
(90 mg/kg/day in divided doses) is recommended. Patients with non-anaphylactic
penicillin allergy may receive cefdinir; macrolides are an alternative for
true penicillin allergy but resistance is rising.

## uti-uncomplicated

For uncomplicated cystitis in non-pregnant women, first-line agents are
nitrofurantoin 100 mg twice daily for 5 days or trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole
160/800 mg twice daily for 3 days where local resistance is below 20 percent.
Fosfomycin 3 g as a single dose is an alternative.

## community-pneumonia

For outpatient community-acquired pneumonia in healthy adults, amoxicillin 1 g
three times daily plus a macrolide or doxycycline is the empiric regimen.
Patients with comorbidities should receive amoxicillin-clavulanate plus a
macrolide, or a respiratory fluoroquinolone.
