# Anticoagulation Guidelines

## monitoring

Patients on warfarin require periodic INR monitoring. The default cadence is
weekly during initiation and after dose changes, then every 4 to 12 weeks once
stable. Patients should avoid cytochrome-inducing or cytochrome-inhibiting
supplements; if such supplements are started the INR must be checked within 3
to 5 days and weekly thereafter until two stable readings are obtained.

## reversal

For major bleeding on warfarin, administer 4-factor prothrombin complex
concentrate (4F-PCC) and intravenous vitamin K. For DOAC reversal, idarucizumab
is the specific reversal agent for dabigatran; andexanet alfa is indicated for
apixaban and rivaroxaban.

## bridging

Bridging anticoagulation with low-molecular-weight heparin is reserved for
patients at very high thromboembolic risk. Recent trial evidence does not
support routine bridging for most atrial fibrillation patients undergoing
elective procedures.
