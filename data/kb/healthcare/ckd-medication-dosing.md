# CKD Medication Dosing

## metformin

Metformin is contraindicated when the estimated glomerular filtration rate
(eGFR) is below 30 mL/min/1.73m^2 due to the risk of lactic acidosis. For eGFR
between 30 and 44 mL/min, the maximum daily dose is 1000 mg with close renal
monitoring. Above 45 mL/min, metformin can usually be continued at standard
doses provided renal function is reassessed every 3 to 6 months.

## sglt2-inhibitors

SGLT2 inhibitors such as empagliflozin and dapagliflozin provide
cardiorenal benefit in CKD. They may be initiated down to an eGFR of 20
mL/min/1.73m^2. Glycaemic efficacy diminishes at lower eGFR but cardiorenal
protection persists.

## raas-blockade

ACE inhibitors and ARBs slow CKD progression in patients with albuminuria. A
serum potassium above 5.5 mmol/L or a creatinine rise of more than 30 percent
within 4 weeks of initiation should prompt dose reduction or discontinuation.
