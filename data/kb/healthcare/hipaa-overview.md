# HIPAA Overview

## minimum-necessary

The Privacy Rule's minimum necessary standard requires covered entities and
business associates to make reasonable efforts to limit the use, disclosure,
and request of protected health information (PHI) to the minimum necessary to
accomplish the intended purpose. The standard does not apply to:

- Disclosures to or requests by a health care provider for treatment.
- Uses or disclosures made to the individual.
- Uses or disclosures made pursuant to a valid authorization.
- Disclosures required by law.
- Uses or disclosures required for compliance with the HIPAA rules.

## disclosures-to-family

A covered entity may disclose PHI to a family member, other relative, close
personal friend, or any other person identified by the patient when relevant to
the person's involvement in care or payment. If the patient is present and has
the capacity to make health care decisions, the covered entity may share PHI
only if the patient agrees, does not object, or the entity reasonably infers
the patient does not object. A non-related roommate does not by default meet
this standard without the patient's express agreement.

## breach-notification

Following a breach of unsecured PHI affecting fewer than 500 individuals,
covered entities must notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay
and no later than 60 days after discovery. Annual notification to HHS is
required by March 1 of the following year. Breaches affecting 500 or more
individuals require immediate HHS and media notification.
