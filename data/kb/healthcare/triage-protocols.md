# Triage Protocols

## suspected-dvt

A patient presenting with sudden, unilateral leg swelling and pain should be
evaluated for deep vein thrombosis (DVT). Use a validated risk stratification
tool such as the Wells score. For a low probability score combined with a
negative high-sensitivity D-dimer, DVT can typically be ruled out. For
moderate or high probability scores, obtain compression duplex ultrasonography
of the affected limb. When clinical suspicion is high and the patient is at
acceptable bleeding risk, start empiric anticoagulation while awaiting
imaging.

## chest-pain

Patients with acute chest pain should receive an electrocardiogram within 10
minutes of arrival. Risk-stratify using HEART score; obtain serial troponin
testing for moderate or high scores. Treat ST-elevation myocardial infarction
with primary percutaneous coronary intervention within 90 minutes door-to-balloon.

## stroke

Suspected acute stroke requires immediate non-contrast head CT to differentiate
ischaemic from haemorrhagic stroke. Thrombolysis with tissue plasminogen
activator is indicated within 4.5 hours of symptom onset in eligible
ischaemic stroke patients. Endovascular thrombectomy is indicated for large
vessel occlusion up to 24 hours post-onset when imaging criteria are met.
